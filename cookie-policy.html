Cookie Policy

How DMTools.fun uses cookies, local storage, analytics, and similar technologies across the website, web app, checkout flow, and Chrome Extension.

Effective Date: April 25, 2026 Last Updated: July 11, 2026

1. Overview

This Cookie Policy explains how DMTools.fun LLC ("DMTools.fun," "we," "our," or "us") uses cookies, browser storage, pixels, tags, analytics tools, and similar technologies when you visit dmtools.fun, use the DMTools.fun web application, interact with our checkout flows, or use related services.

This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and Refund Policy.

Plain-English summary: We use essential technologies to keep the service working, remember user preferences, secure accounts, process payments, and understand website performance. We do not use cookies to automatically send messages for you, and the DMTools Chrome Extension does not collect browsing history or monitor background tabs.

2. What Cookies and Similar Technologies Are

Cookies are small files stored on your device by your browser. Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, pixels, tags, SDKs, server logs, and browser-based identifiers. These technologies can help websites remember login status, preferences, security settings, analytics events, and performance information.

2.1 Types of technologies we may use

  • Session cookies: temporary cookies that usually expire when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies: cookies that remain until they expire or are deleted.
  • Local storage: browser storage used to remember preferences or app state.
  • Analytics tags: tools used to understand traffic, usage, and performance.
  • Server logs: technical logs used for security, diagnostics, abuse prevention, and reliability.

3. Scope: Website, Web App, Checkout, and Extension

3.1 Website and web application

The DMTools.fun website and web application may use cookies and browser storage for authentication, preferences, analytics, security, error handling, and product improvement.

3.2 Chrome Extension

The DMTools Chrome Extension works differently from the website. The extension may use Chrome extension storage or local browser storage to remember user-configured preferences and support functionality. The extension does not collect browsing history, monitor background tabs, or send messages on behalf of users. It only processes content when the user actively uses the extension to help draft a response.

3.3 Checkout and payment pages

Payments are processed by Lemon Squeezy or other authorized payment providers. When you interact with checkout or billing pages, the payment provider may use cookies or similar technologies for fraud prevention, payment completion, tax compliance, security, and account management. Those technologies are governed by the payment provider's own policies.

5. Google Analytics and Measurement

We use Google Analytics on the DMTools.fun website and web application to understand usage, traffic patterns, performance, and product effectiveness. Google Analytics may set cookies such as _ga or related measurement identifiers. Cookie lifetimes may vary depending on browser settings, platform restrictions, user choices, and Google's configuration.

Analytics data helps us understand what pages are used, how users move through the site, whether pages are working properly, and how product improvements perform. We do not use Google Analytics to automatically send messages for users.

5.1 Google Analytics opt-out

You may be able to limit Google Analytics through browser controls, privacy extensions, Google-provided opt-out tools, or any cookie preference controls we make available. Browser privacy features may also shorten or block analytics cookie storage automatically.

Google Advertising and Cookies

DMTools may use Google AdSense to display ads on selected public content pages. Google and other third-party vendors may use cookies to serve ads, and Google advertising cookies may be used to serve ads based on visits to DMTools.fun or other websites.

You can manage or opt out of personalized advertising through Google Ads Settings. Other third-party advertising opt-outs may be available through the YourAdChoices opt-out page. Advertising and personalization choices may vary based on your region and consent settings.

6. Cookies We Do Not Intentionally Use

Unless we update this policy or provide notice, we do not intentionally use cookies to:

  • sell your personal information;
  • automatically send messages or replies on your behalf;
  • collect Chrome Extension browsing history;
  • monitor background tabs through the Chrome Extension;
  • collect keystrokes unrelated to user-initiated DMTools functionality; or
  • serve behavioral advertising based on sensitive personal information.

7.1 Essential technologies

7.2 Non-essential technologies

Where required by law, we will request consent before using non-essential cookies or similar technologies. You may withdraw or change consent through available cookie controls, account settings, browser settings, or device settings.

7.3 Browser controls

Most browsers allow you to block, delete, or limit cookies. You can usually find these controls under privacy or security settings. Blocking all cookies may prevent sign-in, checkout, saved preferences, and other core features from working correctly.

7.4 Global Privacy Control and opt-out preference signals

We review our use of cookies and similar technologies periodically and aim to keep retention no longer than reasonably necessary for the stated purposes.
