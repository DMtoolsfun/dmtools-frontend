Clarity first

How to think about plans versus packs

This is the simple model the new frontend uses. Subscriptions cover ongoing usage. Packs add more responses when you need them.

Pick a subscription if

You expect DMTools to be part of your weekly or monthly workflow and want recurring credits plus feature access built into one plan.

Pick a pack if

You just need extra responses available now, or you want to top up usage without starting a larger recurring plan.

Best first step

If you are new, start free, try the queue or extension, and only upgrade once the workflow clearly saves you time.

What you should always know

Your account should make it obvious how many replies you have left, what plan you are on, and whether you still have pack balance available.

Frequently asked

Questions users should not have to guess about

What is the difference between a subscription and a pack?

A subscription gives you recurring reply allowance and plan features. A pack is a one-time purchase that acts like extra reply balance.

Can I buy a pack without a subscription?

Yes. Packs are meant to work as standalone top-ups or as extra capacity on top of a subscription.

Do I need an account to checkout?

You can check out as a guest by entering an email address. If you are already signed in, checkout uses your existing account context automatically.

Where do I manage plan and pack status?

Your account page should show current plan, replies remaining, billing details, and whether you still have packs available.
